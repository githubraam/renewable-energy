Timeline

A History of Renewable Energy

Renewable energy history spans from prehistoric reliance on biomass and ancient mechanical usage of wind and water to modern electricity generation — a journey thousands of years in the making.

Before 1800s

Ancient & Pre-Industrial Times

  • Biomass (Fire): Early humans used wood and plants for warmth and cooking — the first renewable bioenergy.
  • Wind Power: Used from 5000 B.C.E. in Egypt for sailing ships, and by 200 B.C.E. in China for pumping water.
  • Hydropower: Greeks and Romans used waterwheels for grinding grain and mechanical tasks.
  • Solar & Geothermal: Romans used large windows for solar heating; geothermal energy used for baths.
🌿
⚙️
1800s

The 19th Century & Early Industrialization

  • 1838: First hydrogen fuel cell discovered.
  • 1887–1888: First wind turbines built to generate electricity by James Blyth and Charles Brush.
  • 1891: First commercial geothermal district heating system established.
1900s – 1990s

The 20th Century: Modernizing Renewables

  • 1900s–1950s: Early solar research began; advances in semiconductors and PV cells.
  • 1954: Bell Labs developed the first silicon photovoltaic cell.
  • 1970s Oil Crisis: Oil shortages prompted massive investment in wind, solar, and alternative energy.
  • 1982: India's Ministry of Non-Conventional Energy Sources established.
⚡
🚀
2000s – Present

21st Century: The Energy Transition

  • 2000s–Present: Focus shifted to tackling climate change, resulting in rapid deployment of renewables.
  • 2010s: Creation of large-scale solar farms and improved turbine designs.
  • 2015: Launch of the International Solar Alliance.
  • Future Trends: IEA forecasts renewables to be the largest electricity source by 2027.
Energy Sources

Types of Renewable Energy

Renewable energy comes in many forms, each harnessing natural processes to generate clean, sustainable power for our world.

☀️

Solar Energy

Converts sunlight into electricity via photovoltaic cells. Costs have dropped 89% since 2010, making it one of the cheapest energy sources today.

💨

Wind Energy

Harnesses wind's kinetic energy through turbines. Offshore and onshore wind farms now supply clean power to millions of homes worldwide.

💧

Hydropower

Uses flowing or falling water to generate electricity. The world's largest renewable source, accounting for over 16% of global electricity generation.

🌋

Geothermal

Taps Earth's internal heat for electricity and direct heating. Reliable and consistent — available 24/7 regardless of weather conditions.

🌿

Biomass

Converts organic materials — crops, wood, and waste — into energy. A versatile source for electricity, heat, and transportation fuel.

Why It Matters

Benefits of Renewable Energy

Transitioning to clean energy delivers far-reaching benefits — for people, economies, and the planet we all share.

🌡️

Combats Climate Change

Renewable energy generates little to no greenhouse gas emissions, significantly reducing carbon footprints and helping slow dangerous global warming.

💸

Reduces Energy Costs

Solar and wind costs have plummeted over 80% in the past decade. Renewables now offer the cheapest electricity in history across many regions globally.

🏥

Improves Public Health

Eliminating fossil fuel pollution reduces respiratory and cardiovascular diseases, saving millions of lives and billions in healthcare costs every year.

🔒

Enhances Energy Security

Domestic renewable sources reduce dependence on imported fossil fuels, shielding nations from volatile global energy markets and supply disruptions.

👷

Creates Green Jobs

The renewable energy sector employed over 13 million people in 2022 and is one of the fastest-growing job markets globally across all skill levels.

♾️

Infinite & Inexhaustible

Unlike fossil fuels, renewable sources like sunlight, wind, and water are naturally replenished — we'll never run out, making them truly sustainable forever.
